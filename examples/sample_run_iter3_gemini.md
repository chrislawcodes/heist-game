# Heist Report: The Museum Gala

## Strategy

> I want to run a clean, professional heist with a balanced crew. Pick whichever job fits the crew best — I trust your judgment. Build me a team that can handle whatever comes up: I want at least one specialist in each area we'll need, with backup capability through secondary skills. Risk tolerance: moderate. Pursue bonus opportunities only when they're clearly worth it. Don't take unnecessary chances. If something goes sideways, prioritize getting out clean over maximizing the take. I want a Driver — I don't want to be running on foot.

## Casting

To fulfill your directive for a professional, clean operation, I’ve assembled a crew that prioritizes elite technical proficiency over raw force. I secured **"Slim" Adesanya** (Driver) and **Rook Ferreira** (Safecracker) at their floor costs of $700 each. By locking in 'High' tier specialists for the getaway and the vault work, we ensure that the most critical, high-stress phases of the heist are handled by the best in the business. This leaves us with a high-floor for success where it matters most for a clean exit.

The trade-off for this top-heavy investment was a leaner budget for our support roles. I hired **Sasha Kuznetsova** to handle hacking and **Pearl Sutton** to manage the social and physical gaps. While Sasha is highly competent with medium-tier electronics, Pearl is our 'flex' operative—she provides the necessary Inside Man skills while offering just enough muscle to handle low-threat confrontations without escalating to a shootout.

We are targeting **The Museum Gala**. This job is the best fit because it perfectly aligns Rook’s elite physical skills against 'Hard' security and Sasha’s technical skills against 'Medium' electronics. While the 'Hard' social environment will test Pearl’s capabilities, the job’s 'Low' confrontation profile fits our risk tolerance. Even if a social interaction goes sideways, we have the specialized talent to secure the prize and the elite driving to vanish before things get messy.

## Heist

### Scene 1 — Setup & approach

_**Personnel:** Pearl Sutton, Sasha Kuznetsova · **Reasoning:** For the setup and approach to a high-society gala, we need Pearl's social engineering to talk us through the front door and Sasha's technical support to ensure our digital footprints—and the guest list—look impeccable before we even step out of the car._

The rain slicked the cobblestones of the museum district, reflecting the gala’s searchlights in shimmering, oily pools. Inside the matte-black van, the atmosphere was clinical. Slim sat in the driver’s seat, fingers tapping a rhythmic beat on the steering wheel, his eyes alternating between the rearview mirror and the digital map.

"The perimeter is soft, but the window is tight," Slim said, his voice a low, steady baritone. "I’ve got the engine idling in silent mode. If I see a single light bar on the horizon, the extraction timer starts. Don't make me leave on foot, and don't make me leave without you."

Behind him, Sasha’s face was washed in the blue glow of her monitors. Her fingers moved with mechanical efficiency. "Guest list is updated. Pearl, you’re now 'Ms. Sterling,' an acquisitions consultant from London. I’ve looped the security feed at the North entrance for forty seconds. It’ll look like a momentary stutter—nothing a bored guard will report."

Pearl adjusted the silk lapel of her gown, checking the comms unit hidden behind a diamond earring. She looked every bit the high-society fixture. "Copy that, Sasha. I’ll be the life of the party until I’m not. Keep those eyes on the patrol rotations. I don't like surprises when I’m trying to look expensive."

Rook sat in the shadows of the rear, methodically checking his carbon-fiber bypass tools. He didn't look up; the physical security was his domain, and he was already visualizing the tumblers of the museum's main vault. He knew the weight of every tool, the sound of every click.

"Approaching the drop point," Slim announced as the van slowed. "Sasha, give her the green light."

"Looping now," Sasha whispered. "Go."

Pearl stepped out into the humid night, the van’s door sliding shut with a muffled click. She didn't look back. She walked toward the glowing entrance with the effortless grace of someone who belonged there, leaving the professionals in the dark to wait for the signal.

### Scene 2 — Challenge — social

_**Personnel:** Pearl Sutton, Sasha Kuznetsova · **Reasoning:** Pearl is our primary inside specialist, and though this 'Hard' challenge exceeds her 'Medium' rating, she is the only operative with the requisite social training. Sasha is assigned to assist via comms, using her hacking to provide real-time dossiers and security bypasses that will give Pearl the necessary leverage to navigate the gala's elite social hierarchy. · **Mechanical outcome:** failure_

Pearl glided into the main exhibition hall, Champagne flute in hand, scanning the crowd for her target. The museum director, Arthur Vance, was holding court near a display of rare antiquities.

"Sasha, I have eyes on Vance," Pearl murmured, her lips barely moving behind the rim of her glass.

"Copy that. Pulling his dossier now," Sasha’s voice crackled slightly in Pearl's earpiece. "He’s vain. Likes to talk about his recent acquisitions from private Swiss collectors. Play to his ego."

Pearl approached, flashing a practiced, high-society smile. "Mr. Vance? I'm Ms. Sterling. We share a mutual acquaintance in Geneva who insisted I see your latest addition."

Vance turned, his smile cold and calculating. "Ms. Sterling, is it? Fascinating. My Swiss contacts are usually quite meticulous about formal introductions." He leaned in, his gaze scanning her face with intense scrutiny. "You mentioned Geneva. Which gallery does our mutual acquaintance represent?"

"The Rousseau Gallery," Pearl offered smoothly, repeating the name Sasha had just fed her over the comms.

Vance’s eyes narrowed, the polite facade dropping instantly. "The Rousseau closed three years ago, Ms. Sterling. And their former director is standing directly behind you, by the ice sculpture."

Pearl’s heart skipped a beat. She forced a light laugh to maintain her composure, but the air between them had turned to ice. 

"Sasha," Pearl whispered sharply under her breath. "He’s pushing back. Hard. Give me something else."

"I'm scrambling, Pearl, but his background is heavily scrubbed," Sasha replied, the rhythmic clatter of her keyboard frantic in the earpiece. "I don't have a backup connection. He's not buying the algorithm's generated profile."

"Security!" Vance signaled to two burly men in tailored suits near the archway. "This woman is an imposter. Escort her out and lock down the wing."

The game was up. Pearl’s social engineering was solid, but it was no match for a paranoid mark who intimately knew every player in his elite circle. She set her Champagne flute on a passing tray, her mind racing through emergency protocols. The clean, quiet entry had just become a liability.

"Slim," Pearl said into the comms, dropping her upper-crust accent entirely. "Start the engine. We have a problem."

### Scene 6 — Escape

_**Personnel:** "Slim" Adesanya, Sasha Kuznetsova · **Reasoning:** Slim is our high-tier driver and the anchor of our escape plan; his expertise is essential for a clean getaway. Sasha will provide technical support from the passenger seat, using her hacking skills to manipulate traffic signals and disrupt local police communications to ensure our path remains clear. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

The matte-black van's engine purred with a low, menacing growl as Slim watched the museum's service exit through a rain-streaked mirror. He didn't look at the flashing lights of the patrol cars gathering at the main entrance; his focus was on the physics of the escape route. 

"Thirty seconds until they realize we didn't use the front gates," Slim said, his hands steady on the wheel. "Sasha, tell me you've got the grid."

"Grid is mine, Slim," Sasha replied, her fingers flying across a tablet. Blue light flickered in her eyes as she bypassed the city's traffic management system. "I’m killing the streetlights on the back alley and locking the intersection at 5th and Maine on red. They’ll be chasing ghosts in a parking lot."

The side door hissed open and slammed shut as Pearl and Rook scrambled in. Pearl was breathless, her expensive gown torn at the hem, while Rook remained as impassive as ever, clutching the secured case to his chest. 

"Vance is a paranoid bastard," Pearl spat, clicking her seatbelt into place. "He had a private security detail I didn't see on the floor. Slim, move!"

"I don't move until I'm ready," Slim said calmly, shifting into gear. "And I'm ready."

He didn't floor it; that would have been amateur. Instead, he eased the van into a smooth, rapid acceleration, cutting through the darkened alley with surgical precision. As they hit the main thoroughfare, Sasha tapped a final key. The traffic lights ahead turned a shimmering green just for them, while the intersection behind them became a snarl of confused commuters and blocked police cruisers.

"Police comms are a mess," Sasha reported, a small, satisfied smile touching her lips. "They're reporting a suspect matching Pearl’s description heading toward the harbor. We’re ghosts."

Slim checked the rearview one last time. The museum searchlights were fading into the mist, and the city was opening up before them. "We're clear. No pursuit, no heat. Professional work, everyone. Especially you, Sasha. Those lights were a nice touch."

"Just doing my job, Slim," she replied. "Drive us home."

## Epilogue

The safehouse was quiet, save for the hum of Sasha's cooling servers. On the table, the space where the museum's prize should have been sat glaringly empty.

"Zero payout," Pearl sighed, tossing her ruined silk heels into a corner. "Vance was a paranoid anomaly. I should have read the room faster. That's on me."

Rook meticulously repacked his entirely untouched carbon-fiber bypass tools. "The vault remains closed. A shame. It would have been a beautiful puzzle. But a closed vault is better than a locked cell."

"Exactly," Slim said. He leaned against the concrete wall, nursing a black coffee. "We followed the client's primary directive. When the social angle cracked, we prioritized the exit over the take. No heat, no sirens, no faces on the evening news. We walked away clean."

Sasha nodded, her eyes fixed on scrolling code. "I'm already scrubbing the city's temporary data buffers. By morning, 'Ms. Sterling' will be a ghost."

It wasn't a payday, but in this line of work, surviving a burned op without catching a single charge was its own kind of victory. They lived to pull another job.
