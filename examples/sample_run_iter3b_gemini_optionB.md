# Heist Report: The Corporate Server Farm

## Strategy

> I want to run a clean, professional heist with a balanced crew. Pick whichever job fits the crew best — I trust your judgment. Build me a team that can handle whatever comes up: I want at least one specialist in each area we'll need, with backup capability through secondary skills. Risk tolerance: moderate. Pursue bonus opportunities only when they're clearly worth it. Don't take unnecessary chances. If something goes sideways, prioritize getting out clean over maximizing the take. I want a Driver — I don't want to be running on foot.

## Casting

I’ve assembled a crew that prioritizes technical finesse and a guaranteed exit, staying well within your $2,000 budget at a total spend of $1,700. The core of the strategy relies on the synergy between Sasha Kuznetsova and Jolene "Jo" Hayes; by pairing Sasha’s Medium hacking with Jo’s secondary Low skill, we achieve the High-tier electronic capability needed for the toughest networks without the $1,100 price tag of a master hacker. To fulfill your mandate for a clean getaway, I hired "Slim" Adesanya as our High-tier driver, ensuring we have the best hands behind the wheel regardless of how the job unfolds.

We are targeting "The Corporate Server Farm," a job that perfectly aligns with this team's strengths. The primary obstacle—a Hard Electronic security layer—will be dismantled by our Hacking duo. Meanwhile, Jo handles the Medium Physical security, and Pearl Sutton manages the Social and Muscle requirements at a competent level. This lean, four-person squad covers every entry in the job profile, allowing us to operate with professional precision rather than relying on overwhelming numbers.

The primary trade-off in this selection is our limited combat capacity. By prioritizing technical specialists and a top-tier driver, we have left ourselves with minimal confrontation power; we cannot afford a prolonged shootout. However, by leaving $300 in the bank and selecting a job where confrontation is expected to be Low, we are playing a calculated game. We are betting on a clean, quiet entry and a fast exit, favoring the professional approach you requested over brute force.

## Heist

### Scene 1 — Setup & approach

_**Personnel:** "Slim" Adesanya, Jolene "Jo" Hayes, Sasha Kuznetsova, Pearl Sutton · **Reasoning:** Since this is the initial setup and approach phase without a mechanical challenge, the entire crew is assigned to represent their coordinated arrival at the server farm's perimeter. This deployment ensures everyone is in position for their subsequent specialized tasks while maintaining the professional oversight requested in the strategy._

The nondescript utility van coasted to a halt in the alleyway behind the Silicon Vertex complex, the engine cutting out with a barely perceptible sigh. Rain slicked the pavement, reflecting the sterile, blue glow of the server farm's exterior lighting. Inside the van, the air was tense but strictly professional.

"We are exactly where we need to be," Slim said from the driver's seat, his voice a low, reassuring rumble. He kept his eyes on the side mirror, tracking the distant sweep of a security patrol. "Route is clean. You have a four-minute window before the next rotation. Keep it quiet, keep it boring."

In the back, Pearl adjusted the collar of a high-end, faux-corporate trench coat. "I'll take point on the approach. If anyone asks, I'm the midnight auditor from the parent company, here to pull the Q3 logs. I just need to get us past the perimeter gate and into the loading bay."

Beside her, Sasha was already submerged in the glow of her portable rig, lines of code reflecting in her glasses. "The external perimeter is one thing, but that internal network is a fortress," she murmured, her fingers flying across the keys. She glanced over at Jo. "You ready to ride shotgun on the backdoor protocol? I can't mask our entry alone once we hit the main servers."

Jo meticulously checked the tension wrench in her kit before plugging a braided cable into her own smaller, customized deck. "I'm keyed in, Sasha. I'll handle the physical bypasses on the maintenance doors, and then I'm on your signal to throttle their intrusion countermeasures. We hit them simultaneously."

"Alright, let's make it look easy," Pearl said, sliding the side door open. The crew slipped out into the damp night, moving with practiced precision toward the looming hum of the server farm.

### Scene 2 — Challenge — social

_**Personnel:** Pearl Sutton · **Reasoning:** Pearl Sutton is the designated specialist for social infiltration with a Medium Inside Man skill, which matches the challenge level. No other crew members possess this skill to provide a pairing bonus, so she will handle the entry solo. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

Pearl approached the service entrance with the brisk, annoyed stride of middle management. The rain had picked up, which only added to the authenticity of her irritation. She held her clipboard like a shield and swiped a cloned keycard against the reader. It beeped an angry red.

"Come on," Pearl muttered loudly, slapping the reader with the palm of her hand.

A security guard, a young guy looking bored out of his mind, leaned out of the adjacent booth. "Can I help you, ma'am? That reader's been acting up."

"I certainly hope so," Pearl said, letting out a sharp sigh. "I'm Sutton, external auditing. I'm supposed to be pulling the Q3 physical logs before the Tokyo office wakes up, and my temp badge is apparently a coaster." She checked her watch with an exaggerated flick of her wrist. "If I have to call the regional director at this hour to authorize a manual override, neither of us is going to enjoy the conversation."

On the encrypted comms channel, Slim's voice murmured, "Smooth, Pearl. He's leaning back. Posture's relaxing."

The guard glanced at her tailored coat and the imposing clipboard. Corporate authority was a language of its own, and Pearl spoke it fluently. "Uh, right. Give me one second, Ms. Sutton. Let me just check the expected visitors."

"Check the emergency audit list," Pearl corrected, her tone softening just enough to offer him an out. "It was authorized three hours ago. Look, I just need to get to the sub-basement terminal. You can escort me if it makes you feel better."

The guard hesitated, tapped a few keys on his console, and then shrugged. "No, it's fine. I see a flag for an off-cycle audit. Just sign the physical logbook on your way down." He reached over and hit a heavy green button. The mag-locks on the reinforced door disengaged with a solid *clunk*.

"Appreciate it," Pearl said, her annoyance melting into a curt nod. She pushed through the door. Over the comms, she whispered, "We're inside. Sasha, Jo, you're up."

### Scene 3 — Challenge — electronic

_**Personnel:** Sasha Kuznetsova, Jolene "Jo" Hayes · **Reasoning:** Sasha (Medium Hacker) and Jo (Low Hacker) are paired to elevate their combined skill to High, which is required to successfully bypass a Hard Electronic challenge. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

The sub-basement was cold, dominated by the low, constant hum of the server racks. Sasha and Jo moved quickly to the primary distribution terminal, a massive console protected by biometrics and a nasty piece of adaptive ICE. 

"Alright, this is the hard part," Sasha whispered, unpacking her primary deck and immediately splicing a series of shunts into the terminal's physical interface. "Their firewall is active, and it's already rotating the encryption keys every twelve seconds. If I trip an alarm, they'll lock down the entire grid."

Jo knelt beside her, a smaller, customized terminal resting on her knee. She rapidly tapped out a sequence of commands. "I see it. I'm running a shadow protocol to spoof the biometric handshake. It's a brute-force approach, but it should distract the primary ICE long enough for you to slip past."

"Good. Keep the heat on you," Sasha said, her eyes locked on her screen, reflecting cascading lines of green data. "I'm weaving a false authorization token through the gaps. The encryption is tight... incredibly tight."

Tension stretched thin as the seconds ticked by. Jo's screen flashed yellow warnings. "Sasha, the ICE is adapting. It's noticing the spoof. I can only hold it for another eight seconds before it flags the intrusion."

"Almost..." Sasha muttered, her fingers flying in a blur. "Six seconds. I just need to align the token sequence..."

"Three seconds, Sasha. It's escalating to a priority alert."

"Got it!" Sasha hit the execute key with finality. The terminal's biometric scanner blinked from a hostile red to an inviting green, and the heavy locking mechanism on the server cage released with a soft *hiss*.

Jo let out a breath she didn't realize she was holding, closing her shadow protocol. "Clean bypass. We're invisible on their end."

"Perfect synchronization," Sasha nodded, packing her deck away. "The electronic perimeter is down. The servers are ours."

### Scene 4 — Challenge — physical

_**Personnel:** Jolene "Jo" Hayes · **Reasoning:** Jo Hayes is our only safecracker. While the relocated server has resulted in a more complex physical security layout than initially briefed, she is the only member of the crew with the necessary training to attempt the bypass. · **Mechanical outcome:** failure_

The executive office was a palace of glass and mahogany, but the server wasn't on a standard rack. Instead, it was housed in a custom-integrated 'Aegis-7' vault-cabinet, a model Jo had only seen in high-end trade journals. She knelt before it, her diagnostic tools chirping in a frantic, dissonant rhythm.

"Talk to me, Jo," Pearl’s voice crackled over the comms, tense and low. "Security just started a floor-sweep ahead of schedule. We’re out of time."

"This isn't the layout from the blueprints," Jo whispered, her brow slick with sweat. She had the outer casing off, but the secondary tumbler system was reinforced with a counter-vibration plate she couldn't stabilize. "The physical tolerances are too tight. If I apply more torque, the dead-man switch will fry the drives and alert the entire building."

Sasha’s voice cut in from the sub-basement. "I'm seeing a power spike in your sector, Jo. The cabinet is drawing from a dedicated line. I can’t ghost the alarm if that circuit breaks."

Jo bit her lip, her hands hovering over the delicate internal mechanisms. She tried one last delicate probe, a shim she hoped would bypass the pressure sensor, but the Aegis-7 hissed—a tiny, sharp release of pressurized gas that indicated the lock was shifting into its secondary 'hard-bind' state. 

"Dammit!" Jo hissed, pulling back as the internal tumblers seized. "It's locked out. I can't clear the last relay without the master key. It's a wall. A complete physical wall."

"Jo, we've got movement in the hall!" Pearl urged. 

Jo stared at the silent, impenetrable cabinet for a fraction of a second, the weight of the failure heavy in her chest. "The target is secure. I can't get in. We're done here. Abort. I repeat, abort the job!"

### Scene 6 — Escape

_**Personnel:** "Slim" Adesanya · **Reasoning:** Slim was hired specifically for his High-tier driving expertise to ensure a clean exit. With the heist aborted and the crew needing to disappear quickly, he is the only one qualified to handle the getaway vehicle. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

The back door of the panel van hadn't even fully clicked shut before Slim had the vehicle in gear. The tires chirped against the damp asphalt, but he didn't floor it. He moved with a cold, terrifyingly smooth precision, keeping the headlights off until they cleared the first intersection.

"Everyone down," Slim commanded, his eyes fixed on the rearview mirror. "We're invisible as long as we don't give them a reason to look. Pearl, status on the pursuit?"

Pearl, still breathing hard from the sprint across the loading bay, peered through the small, reinforced rear window. "Nothing. The building's internal sirens are screaming, but they haven't put it together yet. No flashing lights in the perimeter."

In the back, Jo was slumped against a stack of crates, her hands still trembling from the failed attempt on the Aegis-7. "I'm sorry. The physical specs... they weren't what we were told. It was a brick wall."

"Save it for the debrief, Jo," Sasha said, her voice steady despite the adrenaline. She was already wiping the temporary logs from her deck. "We're out. That’s the priority. Slim, take the industrial bypass. Avoid the main tolls."

Slim nodded, flicking the headlights on as they merged into the late-night traffic of the industrial district. He drove with a calm that bordered on the mechanical, weaving through the lanes without a single jerky movement. To any observer, they were just another delivery van finishing a late shift. 

"We're hitting the secondary swap point in three minutes," Slim announced, his voice devoid of any judgment about the failed job. "Clean plates, clean car. By the time they check the sub-basement cameras, we'll be halfway across the county."

"Good," Pearl said, finally leaning back and closing her eyes. "The take was zero, but we’re all breathing. That was the contract."

Slim didn't respond, his focus entirely on the road ahead. He made one final, sharp turn into a shadowed warehouse district, and the Corporate Server Farm—and the failure left behind in its executive suite—faded into the rainy night.

## Epilogue

The safehouse in the industrial district was quiet, smelling of stale coffee and damp rain gear. The crew sat around a metal table, the silence punctuated only by the hum of Sasha’s rig as she scrubbed their digital footprints.

"An Aegis-7," Jo muttered, tossing a lockpick onto the table. "I should have recognized the modified casing on the blueprints. It was a bait-and-switch."

"We rely on the intel we buy," Pearl said, pouring a cup of coffee. "Sometimes it's bad. The important part is that we didn't push a losing hand. We recognized the wall, and we walked away."

Slim stood by the window, peering out through the blinds into the early morning gloom. "A professional knows when to fold," he rumbled softly. "We left with zero, but we left clean. No heat, no casualties, no trace."

The Corporate Server Farm had beaten them tonight, keeping its secrets locked away. But they were alive, their freedom intact, and the city was full of other vaults. Sasha snapped her laptop shut. "Next time, we get better intel."
