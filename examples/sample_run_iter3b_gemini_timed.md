# Heist Report: The Corporate Server Farm

## Strategy

> I want to run a clean, professional heist with a balanced crew. Pick whichever job fits the crew best — I trust your judgment. Build me a team that can handle whatever comes up: I want at least one specialist in each area we'll need, with backup capability through secondary skills. Risk tolerance: moderate. Pursue bonus opportunities only when they're clearly worth it. Don't take unnecessary chances. If something goes sideways, prioritize getting out clean over maximizing the take. I want a Driver — I don't want to be running on foot.

## Casting

I've assembled a lean, versatile crew of four for $1400, leaving a $600 reserve for contingencies. The selection strategy focuses on synergy over the brute force of expensive specialists. Sasha and Jo form our technical vanguard; while neither is a top-dollar hacker alone, their combined expertise provides the High-tier electronic coverage required to crack the most sophisticated server security. Pearl and Margot handle the social layer, pairing their skills to ensure we have a High-tier presence inside the facility, with Margot pulling double duty as our professional wheelman.

We are committing to 'The Corporate Server Farm.' This job perfectly aligns with our specific skill overlaps—the other available contracts presented physical or confrontation hurdles that our current roster couldn't guarantee with professional certainty. The primary trade-off is our light footprint in raw muscle; we are banking on Jo's precision and Pearl's social engineering to bypass trouble rather than fight through it. If the situation degrades, we are relying entirely on Margot's driving to execute a clean exit before the heat can settle.

## Heist

### Scene 1 — Setup & approach

_**Personnel:** Pearl Sutton, Margot Vinter · **Reasoning:** Pearl (Medium Inside Man) and Margot (Low Inside Man) pair to achieve High Social coverage. For the server farm's initial approach, social engineering and professional placement are the safest ways to establish our presence and clear the Medium Social threshold with a professional margin of error._

The matte-black sedan rolled to a silent halt just outside the shadow of the server farm’s cooling towers. Inside, Pearl adjusted the collar of her starch-white blouse, her eyes fixed on the security kiosk. "Margot, check the clock. We’re exactly three minutes ahead of the shift change. Just like we rehearsed."

Margot kept her hands at ten and two, her expression a mask of professional boredom. "I don’t like being early, Pearl. Early is just another word for 'conspicuous.' But the perimeter is soft. No extra patrols, no sudden jitters from the gate guards." She tapped her earpiece. "Sasha, Jo, we’re in position. Give us the green light."

Sasha’s voice crackled in their ears, steady and cool. "The manifest has been updated. You’re currently registered as 'Ventilation Logistics.' I’ve looped the south-facing camera for the next ninety seconds. Move now, or we’re going to have to find a new way to explain why a luxury sedan is parked in a loading zone."

"On it," Pearl said, grabbing a sleek tablet from the dashboard. She stepped out into the humid air, her stride confident, radiating the aura of a woman who was far too busy to be questioned. Behind her, Margot exited the driver’s side, moving with the efficient grace of a seasoned professional, carrying a heavy-duty equipment case that looked far more official than it actually was.

Jo’s voice chimed in, hushed but urgent. "Remember, Pearl, don’t get too close to the readers yet. I’m still scraping the encryption keys from the secondary relay. Just get us through that door."

Pearl didn't look back. She caught the eye of the guard at the kiosk, flashed a brief, annoyed smile, and held up her badge. "Morning, Hector. Logistics said the HVAC sensors in sector four were red-lining. We’re here to make sure your servers don't turn into expensive space heaters."

Margot followed a step behind, her eyes scanning for exits, already calculating the fastest route back to the driver’s seat. The setup was clean. Now, the real work began.

### Scene 2 — Challenge — social

_**Personnel:** Pearl Sutton, Margot Vinter · **Reasoning:** Pearl brings Medium inside_man skills, which alone meets the threshold, but pairing her with Margot's Low inside_man bumps their combined effective rating to High. This guarantees we clear the Medium social challenge cleanly with zero unnecessary risk, perfectly matching the player's cautious, professional strategy. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

The lobby of the server farm was a cathedral of glass and humming climate control. Pearl didn’t break stride as they approached the secondary security tier, where a supervisor named Miller sat behind a reinforced desk, nursing a lukewarm coffee and a deep sense of suspicion. 

"The HVAC ticket?" Miller asked, his eyes darting between Pearl’s face and the tablet she held. "I wasn't told about any logistics check today. Especially not for sector four."

Pearl let a flicker of professional irritation cross her face. "Logistics doesn’t talk to Security, Miller. They just talk to the billing department. If we don’t calibrate the sensors in the next twenty minutes, the failsafes are going to trigger a liquid nitrogen purge. Do you want to be the one explaining why three million dollars in hardware just got flash-frozen?"

Margot stepped forward exactly as rehearsed, shifting the heavy equipment case with a practiced groan of effort. "Look, Pearl, if he doesn't want the signatures, we can just go back to the van. I've got a three-o'clock in Jersey anyway." She looked at Miller, her voice dropping into a tone of weary, blue-collar solidarity. "Honestly, man, help us out. We've been at this since 5:00 AM. Sign the damn log so we can get this over with."

Miller looked at the case, then at Margot’s calloused hands, and finally back at Pearl’s impatient scowl. The combination of Pearl’s high-level bureaucratic pressure and Margot’s grounding, authentic presence was a masterclass in social engineering. The supervisor’s suspicion crumbled under the weight of two professionals who clearly just wanted to do their jobs and go home.

"Fine, fine," Miller muttered, swiping his card through the reader and stamping the digital manifest. "Sector four is through the double doors. Keep your badges visible at all times."

"Appreciate it, Miller," Pearl said, her voice softening just enough to be gracious but not suspicious. As they stepped through the heavy doors, Margot’s voice came over the private channel, barely a whisper. "High-tier, Pearl. He didn't even look at the serial numbers on the case."

"That's why we pair up, Margot," Pearl replied, her eyes already scanning the server racks. "Professionalism is the best camouflage."

### Scene 3 — Challenge — electronic

_**Personnel:** Sasha Kuznetsova, Jolene "Jo" Hayes · **Reasoning:** This is a Hard electronic challenge that requires a High rating to bypass. By pairing Sasha (Medium Hacker) with Jo (Low Hacker), we act at one level higher than the better specialist—hitting High. This synergy is exactly why this crew was built; it clears the farm's hardest security layer without needing an expensive $1100 specialist. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

Deep in the heart of Sector Four, the air was cold and smelled of ozone. Sasha sat cross-legged on the floor in front of the main terminal, her fingers flying across the keys of a portable deck. Above her, the server racks pulsed with an aggressive, rhythmic blue light—the digital heartbeat of a Hard-coded firewall.

"It’s not just a standard encryption gate," Sasha whispered, her brow furrowed. "It’s a cascading watch-dog. Every time I probe a port, it re-routes the traffic through a secondary authentication node. I can’t get a clean lock."

Jo knelt beside her, pulling a small, customized interface tool from her belt. "It’s a physical-layer logic trap, Sasha. Look at the sub-cycle on the third rack. They’ve got a hardware-level ping that bypasses the software gate. You’re fighting the ghost in the machine, but I can see the machine itself."

Jo plugged her tool into a service port at the base of the rack. "I'm going to feed the hardware a false 'Maintenance Mode' signal. It’ll create a three-second window where the cascading logic won't see your probes as an intrusion. It’s a low-level trick, but if we time it right..."

"That's all I need," Sasha said, her expression hardening into a look of absolute focus. "On your mark, Jo. Give me the window."

"Mark!" Jo snapped.

Sasha didn't hesitate. She executed a pre-written script that surged through the gap Jo had created. The blue lights on the racks flickered, then turned a steady, submissive green. The cascade halted, the watch-dog silenced. The combined effort of a software prodigy and a master of the physical mechanism had bypassed a system that would have stopped either of them cold.

"We're in," Sasha breathed, the glow of the terminal reflecting in her eyes. "The core data is exposed. Jo, that hardware catch was brilliant."

"I just like knowing how things are built, Sasha," Jo replied, already packing her gear. "Helps me figure out how to break them. Pearl, Margot—the digital vault is open. Move to the extraction point."

### Scene 4 — Challenge — physical

_**Personnel:** Jolene "Jo" Hayes, Pearl Sutton · **Reasoning:** Jo (Medium Safecracker) handles the vault-specific mechanics, while Pearl (Low Muscle) provides the necessary support for the 'structural' complications introduced by the executive office layout. Since 'structural' is explicitly defined as part of the Physical area, their synergy elevates the team's performance to High, allowing us to clear this Hard challenge as a professional unit. · **Mechanical outcome:** failure_

The executive’s office was a shrine to mahogany and hubris, but behind a sliding Mondrian print lay the reality: a reinforced steel wall housing the server core. Jo was already on her knees, stethoscope pressed to the cold metal, her fingers dancing across a digital bypass rig. Pearl stood guard by the door, her hand resting on the heavy marble statuette she’d claimed as an improvised tool for the structural work they’d anticipated.

"It’s not a standard tumble-lock, Pearl," Jo hissed, her voice tight with rising frustration. "The move to the office wasn't just a layout change. They’ve upgraded to a thermal-reactive plate. If I apply the wrong pressure, the internal mechanism fuses. I need more leverage on the housing just to see the pins."

Pearl moved in, bracing her shoulder against the wall and using a pry-bar to shift the structural casing, trying to give Jo the clearance she needed. "I’m giving you everything I’ve got, Jo. But the wall is reinforced with high-density poly-mesh. It’s not shifting. This isn't a vault; it's a tomb."

Jo’s forehead was beaded with sweat. She adjusted the frequency on her rig, listening for the telltale click of the secondary relay. Seconds ticked by—precious time bleeding away. Suddenly, a sharp, electronic whine echoed through the office. A red light pulsed once, twice, on the bypass rig.

"Dammit!" Jo pulled back, her hands shaking. "The thermal sensor triggered. The secondary locks just dead-bolted. I can't clear this, Pearl. Not without a high-frequency resonator I don't have. The specs Sasha pulled... they were for the old basement layout. This thing is a generation ahead of us."

Pearl looked at the locked wall, then at the security monitor on the desk. "We’re out of time. The failsafe just pinged the main desk. We’re not getting that server today."

"I could try one more—" Jo started, but Pearl gripped her arm.

"No. The boss said prioritize getting out clean. We walked into a wall, Jo. We leave now, or we leave in zip-ties."

### Scene 6 — Escape

_**Personnel:** Margot Vinter · **Reasoning:** This is entirely Margot's domain. As our dedicated driver with a Medium rating, she is tasked with executing the escape plan. Since no one else on the crew possesses driver or related vehicle operation skills to pair with her, she takes the wheel alone, relying on her professional experience to get everyone out clean after the failed breach. · **Mechanical outcome:** success_

The stairwell echoed with the frantic but controlled rhythm of boots on concrete. Pearl led the way, her eyes fixed on the exit signs, while Jo followed, her expression a mix of shame and lingering adrenaline. 

"I could have tried the bypass again," Jo muttered, her voice tight. "If I'd just had another minute to—"

"You had the minutes, Jo," Pearl interrupted, pushing through the heavy fire door into the humid parking garage. "The hardware didn't match the intel. We don't die for bad data. Get in the car."

The matte-black sedan was already idling in the shadow of a concrete pillar. Margot sat as still as a statue, her hands light on the wheel, her gaze flicking between the rearview mirror and the gatehouse. As the two women dove into the seats, Sasha looked up from her glowing laptop in the back. 

"Digital traces are ghosted," Sasha said, her voice a cool contrast to the tension in the car. "The server farm’s internal logs will show a routine maintenance check and a sensor glitch. No alarms until they physically check the office tomorrow morning."

"Good," Margot said. It was the only word she’d spoken since they arrived. She shifted into gear, the tires chirping once as she pulled out of the bay. "The gate is still open. They haven't called the lockdown yet."

As they approached the exit, the security booth guard looked up, waving them through with the same bored indifference he'd shown on their way in. Margot accelerated smoothly, merging into the late-night traffic of the industrial district with the invisibility of a professional who knew exactly how to blend in.

"We missed the take," Jo said, leaning her head back against the seat and closing her eyes.

"We stayed clean," Pearl replied, looking out at the passing city lights. "That’s the job, Jo. The money’s only worth it if you’re not in a cage to spend it. Margot, take us to the secondary drop. We’re done here."

Margot didn't respond; she just adjusted the mirror and pushed the car a little harder, the engine’s low growl the only sound in the quiet, professional interior. They were out. They were safe. And in this business, that was a victory of its own.

## Epilogue

The safehouse was quiet, smelling of stale coffee and damp brick. There was no stack of bearer bonds to count, no celebratory drinks to pour. Just the soft hum of Sasha’s laptop as she scrubbed the last of their digital footprint from the city's grid.

Jo sat at the table, methodically breaking down her bypass rig. "A thermal-reactive plate," she grumbled, tossing a fused wire into the trash. "If we'd known the server was moved, I would have packed the nitrogen."

"Ifs don't pay the rent, but they keep you out of lockup," Pearl said, pouring a cup of black coffee. She looked around the room, taking in the exhausted but free crew. "The intel was dirty, and we hit a wall. But we stuck to the plan. We didn't panic, we didn't force a bad hand, and Margot drove us out without triggering a single flashing light."

Margot leaned against the doorframe, keys still spinning idly around her finger. "A zero-dollar payday beats a ten-year sentence," she said, pocketing the keys. "Get some sleep. We'll find a better mark tomorrow."
