# Character Profile Template

Mechanical fields (`id`, `name`, `skills`, `floor_cost`) are locked per the
design doc. The eight descriptive fields below drive bidding decisions,
scene assignment, narration, dialogue, and portrait generation.

Target ~80–150 words total per character when fully filled in.

---

## `backstory` — where they came from
*1–2 sentences. Where they're from, how they got their skill.*

1. Did six years for hacking a Cleveland bank at twenty-two. Got out, did corporate pen-testing for a decade, fell back into the work when his contract dried up.
2. Born into a Vegas casino family; learned to crack safes before she could drive. The family business stopped being family when her older brother flipped state.
3. Twenty years a stunt driver in Toronto until a producer skipped town with his pay. A friend with worse debts introduced him to a better-paying audience.

## `voice` — how they talk
*1 sentence. Cadence, vocabulary, tics. The most important field for narration.*

1. Short, deliberate phrases. Never uses contractions. Pauses before names like she's deciding whether to say them.
2. Drawls like a late-night radio host. Buries jokes inside operational chatter. Calls everyone "partner" regardless of how he feels about them.
3. Clipped, surgical sentences. Drops articles. Sounds like someone reading traffic reports at three a.m.

## `motivation` — why they keep doing this
*1 sentence. What they're playing for. Drives risk and decision behavior.*

1. Saving for her mother's stroke care. The house is mortgaged twice; the third refusal letter came last month.
2. Believes a clean exit is the only ending that matters. Doesn't care about the money beyond proving the cops can't catch him.
3. Putting his daughter through Stanford on the lie that he's a freelance consultant. She's a junior. Two more years.

## `quirk` — a memorable habit
*1 sentence. A physical tic the AI can echo in scene text.*

1. Always carries a worn-out poker chip from a tournament he never entered. Flips it between his fingers when thinking.
2. Hums a single hymn from her childhood — never the whole thing, always the same four bars — before doing anything dangerous.
3. Polishes his eyeglasses with a microfiber cloth after every conversation, including ones over the radio.

## `crew_dynamic` — how they treat teammates
*1 sentence. Gives the AI dialogue material for in-scene exchanges.*

1. Treats the crew like a unit she's the sergeant of. Shares the take but not the command. Calls the youngest member "kid" regardless of age.
2. Mostly quiet on jobs. Carries water bottles and granola bars for everyone. Doesn't speak unless directly asked.
3. Cracks jokes constantly, mostly at his own expense. Disappears into himself the moment things actually go wrong.

## `weakness` — what shakes them
*1 sentence. Useful for failure scenes and decision points.*

1. Hates open spaces. If she has to stand exposed for more than ten seconds, her hands start to shake.
2. Refuses to point a gun at anyone. Will defer to whoever else is willing to.
3. Sees red whenever a cop is hurt. Will abort a job to avoid that line, no matter the cost.
